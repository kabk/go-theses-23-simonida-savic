ARE YOU HIGH YET?

abstract

Magic mushrooms, tripping, shrooms, truffles, blue meanies, mushies, are all terms that we might understand in (western) society as synonyms for the psychedelic drug psilocybin. As this topic is becoming more known in the western societies mainstream media more for its beneficial qualities rather than for its old stigma with fear and misconception, there is something interesting happening with the current anatomy of the psychoactive drug in today's context. By starting from a brief overview on this small organism, the mushroom itself, I'd like to dissect and cut the mushroom into different parts: Why would these organisms be important enough to spend our time with, ingesting and sharing new knowledges about them? Where do the psilocybin species come from and when did we start cultivating them in the West?

In this thesis I'm interested in the way how mushrooms influence western culture right now, but also where the roots travel back in history. To widen the scope, I'd like to slightly touch upon various aspects, such as the psychoactive medical studies in the early 50's — that were quite important for the development and understanding of the chemical compound that somehow works inside the human brain; how psilocybin can offer us new perspectives, crack open our biggest fears and still offer us enlightenment and a greater sense of consciousness. New doors have been opened for the development of treating mental health problems, that even stretch as far as to outer space. Nowadays’ (western) societies seem to be more exhausted, stressed and detached from their well-being and nurtured minds. I would like to peel each layer from mushroom, unfolding its great presence amongst us, even though sometimes this is still being neglected and overlooked by a negative bias and prejudices within our (western) society.
